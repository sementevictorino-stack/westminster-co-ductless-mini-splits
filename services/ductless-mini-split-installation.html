Refrigerant Line Installation

Proper refrigerant line installation is critical for ductless mini split system performance, efficiency, and reliability. Our certified technicians use copper tubing sized according to manufacturer specifications and industry standards to ensure optimal refrigerant flow and heat transfer.

The line installation process begins with careful routing planning to minimize length while avoiding obstacles and maintaining proper support. We use pre-insulated line sets when possible to reduce installation time and ensure consistent insulation quality. All tubing is protected from mechanical damage and properly insulated to prevent condensation and energy loss.

Our technicians use proper tube bending techniques and specialized tools to create smooth, gradual bends that minimize pressure drop and turbulence. All connections are made with flaring tools calibrated for precise fit and leak-tight seals. We use nitrogen purging during brazing operations to prevent oxidation and contamination of refrigerant circuits.

After line installation, we perform comprehensive leak testing using electronic leak detectors and pressure testing with nitrogen to ensure system integrity. All refrigerant lines are properly evacuated to remove moisture and air before charging with refrigerant according to manufacturer specifications.

Electrical Installation and Code Compliance

All electrical work associated with ductless mini split installation is performed by certified electricians in compliance with National Electrical Code (NEC) requirements and local electrical codes. We ensure proper overcurrent protection, grounding, and disconnect switches for safe and reliable operation.

The electrical installation process includes evaluation of existing electrical service capacity to ensure adequate power supply for the new ductless mini split system. When necessary, we coordinate with licensed electricians to upgrade electrical service or add dedicated circuits to support system requirements.

Low-voltage control wiring between indoor and outdoor units is installed using manufacturer-specified cable types and routing methods. We ensure proper separation from power wiring to prevent interference and follow manufacturer guidelines for wire management and termination.

All electrical connections are made with appropriate connectors and protection methods to ensure long-term reliability. We provide proper labeling of circuits and disconnects to facilitate future service and maintenance activities.
